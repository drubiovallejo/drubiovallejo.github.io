About

I'm a PhD graduate from the Department of Linguistics and Cognitive Science of the University of Delaware, where my advisor was Satoshi Tomioka.

My research concentrates on the interface between the syntax, semantics and pragmatics of natural language, where I look at the interaction between circumstantial modality, tense, aspect, and negation. In my dissertation, Modal non-assertions , I investigate a range of unexpected meanings (e.g. actuality effects, counterfactuality presuppositions) that arise with respect to the prejacents of root modals under specific configurations of the aforementioned functional heads.

Beyond modal phenomena, I'm puzzled by the behavior of bare singular count nouns and their licensing conditions.

I also enjoy using formal languages to model and process natural ones [some code].
